🧩 Grammar warm-up: "I feel…" and "I have a…"

10 min

Two simple patterns cover almost everything she'd need to say about how she's feeling — we drill both before the rest of the lesson.

Quick drill: fill the gap
🗣️ Oral drill: say the full sentence

Read the cue out loud, then she says the full sentence. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "I feel tired." → (sick) → "I feel sick."
  2. "I have a headache." → (a cold) → "I have a cold."
  3. "My throat is sore." → (my back) → "My back is sore."
  4. "She feels dizzy." → (he) → "He feels dizzy."
  5. "My back hurts." → (my head) → "My head hurts."
