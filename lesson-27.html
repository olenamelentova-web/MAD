Lesson 27 · 90 min · Unit 3

Emotions & Reactions

Reacting naturally to news, good and bad — the difference between sounding flat and sounding genuinely engaged. Grammar focus: exclamations with That's so…! and How…!, used throughout every task below.

🧩 Grammar warm-up: exclamations

10 min

"That's so…!" and "How…!" turn a flat statement into a genuine reaction — small words that make a huge difference to how engaged she sounds.

🗣️ Oral drill: react out loud

Read the cue out loud, then she gives the full reaction. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the reaction, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "That's amazing!" → (wonderful) → "That's wonderful!"
  2. "How exciting!" → (surprising) → "How surprising!"
  3. "I'm so proud of you." → (happy for you) → "I'm so happy for you."
  4. "Oh no, that's terrible." → (awful) → "Oh no, that's awful."
  5. "I'm really nervous about it." → (worried) → "I'm really worried about it."
