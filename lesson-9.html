Describing People & Things

Vocabulary and structures for describing appearance, personality, and everyday objects. Grammar focus: adjectives and comparatives (taller than, more beautiful), used throughout every task below.

🧩 Grammar warm-up: comparatives

10 min

Short adjectives add "-er" (tall → taller). Longer adjectives use "more" instead (beautiful → more beautiful). We drill both patterns before they show up in every task today.

🗣️ Oral drill: comparison swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again adding the comparison — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "She is tall." → (taller than me) → "She is taller than me."
  2. "This is good." → (better than that) → "This is better than that."
  3. "He is nice." → (nicer than his brother) → "He is nicer than his brother."
  4. "This bag is big." → (bigger than mine) → "This bag is bigger than mine."
  5. "Today is bad." → (worse than yesterday) → "Today is worse than yesterday."
