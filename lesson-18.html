Asking for Help & Giving Advice

Two directions of the same skill: asking for support, and offering it. Grammar focus: You should…, Why don't you…?, Have you tried…?, used throughout every task below.

🗣️ Oral drill: say the full sentence

Read the cue out loud, then she says the full sentence. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "You should rest." → (see a doctor) → "You should see a doctor."
  2. "Why don't you try tea?" → (call her) → "Why don't you call her?"
  3. "Have you tried resting?" → (this medicine) → "Have you tried this medicine?"
  4. "You should ask for help." → (relax) → "You should relax."
  5. "Why don't you take a break?" → (go home) → "Why don't you go home?"
