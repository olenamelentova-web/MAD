Celebrations & Holidays

Talking about traditions — both Ukrainian and Irish — and mixing habits with specific memories. Grammar focus: present simple for traditions ("we usually…") + past simple for specific years ("last year we…"), used throughout every task below.

🧩 Grammar warm-up: habits vs. specific memories

10 min

"We usually…" describes what always happens. "Last year we…" describes one specific time. Mixing the two is what makes talking about traditions sound natural.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "We usually celebrate at home." → (my sister's house) → "We usually celebrate at my sister's house."
  2. "Last year we visited Ann." → (my sister) → "Last year we visited my sister."
  3. "We always decorate the tree." → (light candles) → "We always light candles."
  4. "Last Christmas was special." → (birthday) → "Last birthday was special."
  5. "We usually give gifts." → (make a toast) → "We usually make a toast."
