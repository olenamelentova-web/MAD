Travel & Getting Around

Airport and transport English — lower frequency but high-stakes once a year. Grammar focus: How do I get to…? and Can you tell me…?, used throughout every task below.

🧩 Grammar warm-up: asking for directions

10 min

One question shape covers almost every "I'm lost" moment — we drill it and its natural answers first.

Match it: question → matching answer

Click a question on the left, then the answer that fits it on the right.

Quick drill: how, can, on, or left?
🗣️ Oral drill: ask the question

Read the cue out loud, then she asks the full question. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the question, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "How do I get to gate five?" → (the exit) → "How do I get to the exit?"
  2. "Can you tell me where the toilet is?" → (the café) → "Can you tell me where the café is?"
  3. "Is it far?" → (close) → "Is it close?"
  4. "Where is the taxi rank?" → (the bus stop) → "Where is the bus stop?"
  5. "How much is a ticket?" → (a coffee) → "How much is a coffee?"
