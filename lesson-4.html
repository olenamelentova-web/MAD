Shopping & Supermarket Survival English

🧩 Grammar warm-up: the three supermarket questions

10 min

Almost everything she needs to say in a shop is one of three question shapes. We drill the shapes and the answers first, before she has to produce them live.

Match it: question → matching answer

Click a question on the left, then the answer that fits it on the right.

Quick drill: where, do, how, or can?
🗣️ Oral drill: ask the question

Read the cue out loud, then she asks the full question — no writing, flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the question, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing to think between them.

  1. "Where is the milk?" → (bread) → "Where is the bread?"
  2. "Do you have bananas?" → (apples) → "Do you have apples?"
  3. "How much is this?" → (that) → "How much is that?"
  4. "Can I pay by card?" → (cash) → "Can I pay by cash?"
  5. "Where can I find the cheese?" → (butter) → "Where can I find the butter?"

🗣️ Speaking practice

Core phrase drill

🧠 Interactive task 1: Supermarket logic quiz

10 min

Read each mini-scenario aloud together, then she answers out loud before clicking. This checks she can pick the right phrase in the moment, not just recite vocabulary.

🔤 Interactive task 2: Sentence builder

10 min

Click the word chips in the correct order to build each sentence, then hit Check. Say the finished sentence out loud before moving to the next one.

🏷️ Interactive task 3: Price match

8 min

Click an item on the left, then click its price on the right. Say the full sentence out loud before clicking: "The milk is one twenty."

🗺️ Interactive task 4: Which aisle?

8 min

She answers out loud first, then clicks. This checks she knows where things actually are, not just how to ask.
