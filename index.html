The Route

👤 Learner profile

  • Understands more than she can produce — translates in her head before speaking
  • Pronunciation is shaky; grammar basics known but inconsistent
  • Uses English mainly with her daughter's Irish boyfriend, and occasionally in supermarkets
  • Priority: get her talking, even imperfectly. Fluency over accuracy.

🧭 Every lesson, same shape

  • Warm-up (5m) — talking from second one
  • Vocabulary (10m) — said aloud, not just read
  • Grammar (5–10m) — minimal, used in spoken sentences
  • Speaking practice (15–20m) — the core of the lesson
  • Roleplay (10–15m) — her real situations
  • Wrap-up (5m) — she summarizes out loud

✎ Keeping her speaking, not translating

  • Wait 2–3 seconds max — resist filling silence, let her search for words in English
  • Model full sentences, not single words, so she absorbs real patterns
  • Correct pronunciation after she finishes speaking, never mid-sentence
  • Repeat core phrases across lessons — repetition beats novelty at this level
