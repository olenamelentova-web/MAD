Lesson Plan

All 30 lessons, grouped into 3 units of 10. Click any lesson title to open it directly.

👤 Learner profile

  • Understands more than she can produce — translates in her head before speaking
  • Pronunciation is shaky; grammar basics known but inconsistent
  • Uses English mainly with her daughter's Irish boyfriend, and occasionally in supermarkets
  • Priority: get her talking, even imperfectly. Fluency over accuracy.

🧭 What's on every lesson page

  • Grammar warm-up — the structure she'll need, practiced first
  • Vocabulary with pictures — flip cards, click to hear pronunciation
  • Pronunciation task — stress patterns and listen & repeat
  • Interactive tasks — matching, quizzes, sorting, sentence building
  • Roleplay scripts — her real situations, ready to perform

✎ Structure notes

  • Units 1–2 stay tightly practical — her real, immediate situations (the boyfriend, the supermarket, daily life). Unit 3 opens up into general conversational confidence.
  • Every review lesson (10, 20, 30) mixes roleplay from all preceding lessons in that unit — a roleplay marathon, not just recent material.
  • Lesson 30 ends by comparing a fresh recording to her Lesson 1 recording — a nice bookend if you've been collecting those voice notes throughout.
  • Each lesson runs about 90 minutes: grammar warm-up, illustrated vocabulary, pronunciation, speaking practice, 2+ interactive tasks, gap-fills, and two roleplay scripts.
