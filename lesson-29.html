Telling a Longer Story

Turning a list of events into an actual story — the last real skill gap before the final review. Grammar focus: linking words (first, then, after that, because, so), used throughout every task below.

🗣️ Oral drill: link the sentences

Read the two cue sentences out loud, then she joins them with the right linking word. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again swapping in the new linking word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "First, I woke up. Then, I had breakfast." → (after that) → "First, I woke up. After that, I had breakfast."
  2. "I was tired, so I went to bed early." → (because) → "I went to bed early because I was tired."
  3. "Suddenly, it started raining." → (finally) → "Finally, it started raining."
  4. "Meanwhile, my sister was cooking." → (then) → "Then, my sister was cooking."
  5. "Eventually, we found the hotel." → (finally) → "Finally, we found the hotel."
