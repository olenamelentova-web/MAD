🧩 Grammar warm-up: "to be"

10 min

Am, is, are — three tiny words that carry almost every sentence she'll say about herself today. We drill them first.

Match it: pronoun → correct form

Click a pronoun on the left, then the form of "to be" that matches it on the right.

Quick drill: am, is, or are?
🗣️ Oral drill: say the full sentence

Read the cue out loud, then she says the full sentence. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: subject swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again with the new subject — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "I am fifty-four." → (she) → "She is fifty-four."
  2. "You are kind." → (he) → "He is kind."
  3. "We are a family." → (they) → "They are a family."
  4. "I am from Ukraine." → (we) → "We are from Ukraine."
  5. "He is my boyfriend." → (they) → "They are my friends."
