Food & Eating Out

Talk about food preferences and manage a restaurant / café situation. Grammar focus: like / don't like and would like (→ "I'd like"), used throughout every task below.

🧩 Grammar warm-up: "would like" → "I'd like"

10 min

This contraction is what makes her sound natural when ordering — so we drill it first, before it appears in every task today.

Match it: full form → contraction

Click a full form on the left, then its contraction on the right (e.g. "I would like" → "I'd like").

Quick drill: like, likes, don't like, or 'd like?
🗣️ Oral drill: 'd like or like?

Read the cue out loud, then she says the full sentence — "'d like" for a request right now, "like/likes" for a general preference. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: pronoun swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again with the new person in brackets — out loud, back and forth, no pausing to think between them.

  1. "I'd like a coffee." → (she) → "She'd like a coffee."
  2. "We don't like spicy food." → (he) → "He doesn't like spicy food."
  3. "They'd like to order now." → (I) → "I'd like to order now."
  4. "She likes sweet food." → (we) → "We like sweet food."
  5. "He'd like the bill." → (you) → "You'd like the bill."
