Beyond Lesson 30

Recommendations for Further Study

Finishing 30 lessons is a real milestone — not a finish line. Here's how to keep building on it once the course ends.

🗣️ Keep the speaking habit alive

This is the single most important thing on this page. Everything else is secondary to simply continuing to talk.

  • Use it with the people she already has — the boyfriend and Ann are the best practice partners she'll ever find. Encourage a standing "English half-hour" during calls, not just full immersion during visits.
  • Narrate her own day, out loud, in English — even alone. A few minutes of "talking to herself" about her morning keeps production active between real conversations.
  • Record herself weekly, the way the course did in Lessons 1 and 30 — short voice notes on any topic, just to keep the habit of hearing her own spoken English.

🎧 Free listening practice

Aim for a little every day rather than a lot occasionally.

  • BBC Learning English — free, includes "6 Minute English," graded by level, transcripts included
  • British Council LearnEnglish — free listening and reading practice organized by CEFR level
  • Irish radio and podcasts (RTÉ) — since she's specifically adjusting to an Irish accent, exposure to real Irish speech matters more than generic "British English" content
  • Subtitled Irish or British TV — English subtitles (not Ukrainian) force reading + listening together

📖 Reading at the right level

  • Graded readers at A2–B1 level — simplified books written specifically for learners, widely available from ELT publishers
  • News in Levels / Breaking News English — real news stories rewritten at different difficulty levels
  • Children's or YA books she already knows the story of (in translation) — familiarity with plot reduces the cognitive load of new vocabulary

🧠 Vocabulary that actually sticks

  • Spaced repetition apps (e.g. Anki, or any flashcard app with spaced review) — far more effective than re-reading a list once
  • Keep adding to the same vocabulary set the course built rather than starting new lists from scratch — reinforcement beats novelty
  • Focus new vocabulary on upcoming real situations (a specific trip, a specific event) rather than random word lists

👥 Finding more practice partners

  • Conversation exchange apps and websites, where learners of two languages practice with each other
  • Local or online English-conversation groups for adult learners — often free, low-pressure, and social rather than academic
  • Continuing with a tutor for regular structured practice, if the momentum from this course is worth keeping formal

🎯 If she wants a benchmark or certificate

Not necessary for her actual goals, but useful if she'd find a concrete target motivating.

  • Cambridge English Qualifications — A2 Key or B1 Preliminary are natural next steps from where this course leaves her
  • Free practice tests for these exams are widely available and double as excellent, structured revision
  • A concrete exam date can be a strong motivator to keep a regular study rhythm going

✎ The honest advice

  • Consistency beats intensity. Fifteen minutes a day will do more than three hours once a month.
  • Keep talking more than she studies grammar. That was the whole philosophy of this course, and it doesn't stop mattering after Lesson 30.
  • Mistakes are still fine. Fluency, not perfection, was always the goal — that doesn't change now.
