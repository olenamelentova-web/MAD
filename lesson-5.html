Past Simple — What Did You Do Yesterday?

Comfortably talk about recent past events. Grammar focus: past simple (regular and key irregular verbs), used throughout every task below.

10 MIN🧩 Grammar warm-up 15 MIN🗂️ Vocabulary + pictures 10 MIN🔊 Pronunciation 15 MIN🗣️ Speaking practice 10 MIN🧠 Irregular verb quiz 10 MIN🔤 Sentence builder 10 MIN✏️ Fill in the blanks 10 MIN🎭 Roleplay scripts 5 MIN🏠 Wrap-up

🧩 Grammar warm-up: past simple

10 min

A handful of irregular verbs cover most of what she'll ever need to say about yesterday — we drill those first.

Click a base verb on the left, then its past form on the right.

Quick drill: fill the past form
🗣️ Oral drill: say the full sentence

Read the cue out loud, then she says the full past-tense sentence. Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: subject swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again with the new person — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "I went to the shops." → (she) → "She went to the shops."
  2. "We watched TV." → (they) → "They watched TV."
  3. "He bought a present." → (I) → "I bought a present."
  4. "She had breakfast." → (we) → "We had breakfast."
  5. "They met their friends." → (he) → "He met his friends."

🔊 Pronunciation practice

10 min

The "-ed" ending changes sound depending on the verb — this is the single biggest past-simple pronunciation trap. Click the purple button to hear each one, then she repeats it three times.

Tongue twister (read it together, then she tries it alone — three times, a little faster each time)

"She watched, walked, and talked to her worried worker."

🗣️ Speaking practice

15 min
Yesterday timeline

On Miro, she places sticky notes in order along a timeline and narrates her day, start to finish, in the past simple.

Chain story

You say one sentence about yesterday, she continues with the next one — back and forth, building a shared story.

Prompt cards — she answers out loud, flip to check a sample
