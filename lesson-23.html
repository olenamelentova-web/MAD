🧩 Grammar warm-up: there is / there are

10 min

Singular takes "is," plural takes "are" — a small rule that appears constantly when describing any space.

Match it: item → correct form

Click an item on the left, then whether it needs "There is" or "There are" on the right.

Quick drill: is or are?
🗣️ Oral drill: say the full sentence

Read the cue out loud, then she says the full sentence with "there is" or "there are." Flip only to check.

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "There's a sofa in the living room." → (a table) → "There's a table in the living room."
  2. "There are three bedrooms." → (two bathrooms) → "There are two bathrooms."
  3. "There isn't a garden." → (a balcony) → "There isn't a balcony."
  4. "There's a window near the bed." → (a lamp) → "There's a lamp near the bed."
  5. "There are stairs to the second floor." → (a lift) → "There's a lift to the second floor."
