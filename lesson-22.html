🧩 Grammar warm-up: superlatives

10 min

Comparative compares two things ("taller than"). Superlative picks the winner out of a whole group ("the tallest"). We drill the superlative forms first, since they're new.

🗣️ Oral drill: say the full sentence

🗣️ Oral drill: swap chain

Say the sentence, then say it again swapping in the new word — out loud, back and forth, no pausing.

  1. "She's the tallest in the family." → (funniest) → "She's the funniest in the family."
  2. "This is the best coffee." → (worst) → "This is the worst coffee."
  3. "Dublin is the biggest city." → (oldest) → "Dublin is the oldest city."
  4. "He's the most beautiful singer." → (talented) → "He's the most talented singer."
  5. "This is as good as that." → (cheap) → "This is as cheap as that."
